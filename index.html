Game of Life

A cellular automaton devised by mathematician John Conway in 1970. The game follows simple rules to simulate the evolution of a grid of cells, where each cell can be either alive or dead. More...

100ms

Rules

  1. Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbors dies (underpopulation)
  2. Any live cell with two or three live neighbors lives on to the next generation
  3. Any live cell with more than three live neighbors dies (overpopulation)
  4. Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbors becomes a live cell (reproduction)

Instructions

  1. Click on cells to toggle them alive/dead
  2. Use Start/Stop to control the simulation
  3. Adjust speed with the slider
  4. Save interesting patterns
  5. Use step buttons to move frame by frame
